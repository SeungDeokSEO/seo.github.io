**Beyond Economic and Political Motives: Exploring the Religious Dynamics in Gulf States’ Foreign Aid Allocation (with Paul Un)**

**Abstract**: Foreign aid from Kuwait, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, and the United Arab Emirates has often been described as religiously motivated. Previous studies have found aid from Gulf State donors is more often directed toward Muslim recipient nations. However, these studies have been hampered empirically by a focus on Middle Eastern and North African recipients, and hampered methodologically due to their cross-national comparative designs, which obscure the actual sub-national destinations of aid flows. Using novel data from the Lowy Institute, our study asks: Do Gulf States’ foreign aid to Indonesia exhibit patterns of sub-national allocation consistent with social identity theory, in terms of favoring recipients with culturally or religiously similar profiles to the donors? This sub-national survey of extra-regional Gulf State aid flows provides new insights into the aid policies of non-traditional donors.
